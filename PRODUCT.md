# HSE SaaS Platform - Product Definition

## Product Purpose
A comprehensive Health, Safety & Environment (HSE) management platform for construction and industrial companies. The app helps safety managers track incidents, manage work permits, monitor worker certifications, and maintain regulatory compliance across multiple projects.

## Register
**Primary**: Product (app UI, dashboard, tool)  
**Secondary**: Brand (landing page, marketing, acquisition)

## Users

### Primary: Safety Managers (HSE Officers)
- **Context**: On-site at construction projects, often outdoors or in site offices
- **Device**: Laptop and tablet, sometimes checking data on phone while walking sites
- **Lighting**: Varies - bright daylight on site, fluorescent office lighting, sometimes dim site cabins
- **Mood**: Focused, under pressure, needs information fast
- **Goal**: Keep workers safe, maintain compliance, prevent incidents

### Secondary: Project Managers
- **Context**: Office-based, monitoring multiple projects simultaneously
- **Device**: Desktop monitors (27"+), occasional tablet for site visits
- **Lighting**: Office environment, often long hours
- **Mood**: Balancing safety with schedule and budget pressures
- **Goal**: Project delivery with zero incidents, clear documentation

### Tertiary: Workers (read-only)
- **Context**: Accessing from site kiosks or personal phones
- **Device**: Shared tablets or personal smartphones
- **Lighting**: Outdoor, bright sunlight
- **Mood**: Quick check - "Do I have the right permit?"
- **Goal**: Verify their status quickly, access training materials

## Brand Tone
- **Professional but human**: Not corporate sterile, not playful either
- **Trustworthy**: Safety-critical industry needs reliability
- **Confident**: The app knows what it's doing
- **Clear**: No ambiguity when it comes to safety

## Anti-References
- **Avoid**: Finance/crypto dark dashboards with neon accents
- **Avoid**: Generic SaaS "blue gradient everything"
- **Avoid**: Healthcare soft pastels that feel clinical
- **Avoid**: Construction cliches (hard hats, cones, caution tape imagery)

## Strategic Principles
1. **Safety is serious, but the tool doesn't need to be grim**: Use color intentionally, not just "warning yellow"
2. **Information density is fine**: These users are professionals who need data
3. **Speed matters**: Animations should feel responsive, not slow down workflows
4. **Mobile is secondary but real**: Desktop-first design that adapts gracefully
5. **Trust through consistency**: Same patterns, predictable behaviors

## Physical Scene
"A safety manager in a portacabin at 2pm, afternoon sun streaming through dusty windows, checking permit statuses on a 15-inch laptop while coordinating with site supervisors via radio. They need to quickly scan for any red flags, approve a hot work permit, and check which workers' certifications expire this week."

---

## Color Strategy: Committed
One saturated color carries 30-60% of the surface. We'll use a deep, confident blue as our anchor (think maritime/naval trust, not corporate flat blue), paired with warm neutrals that feel human, not sterile.

## Theme Decision
**Light mode default**, with dark mode available. The physical scene (portacabin, afternoon sun) suggests light mode for readability. Dark mode for late-shift work or users who prefer it.
